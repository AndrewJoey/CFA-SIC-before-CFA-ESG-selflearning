## What is CFA-SIC?

The **CFA Institute Certificate in Sustainable Investing & Climate (CFA-SIC)** is a globally recognized professional certificate offered by the CFA Institute. It was previously known as the **CFA ESG Investing Certificate** before its rebranding.

### Why get this certificate?

| Benefit | Details |
|---|---|
| **Industry recognition** | Issued by CFA Institute, one of the most respected financial credentialing bodies worldwide |
| **Career relevance** | ESG and sustainable investing are increasingly required skills in asset management, banking, and corporate finance |
| **Accessible entry point** | No prerequisites — suitable for professionals from finance, law, consulting, or non-finance backgrounds |
| **Practical knowledge** | Covers real-world ESG analysis, portfolio integration, and stewardship practices |

### Who is it for?

- Finance professionals looking to add ESG expertise
- Students preparing to enter the investment industry
- Corporate sustainability practitioners moving into finance roles
- Anyone curious about how environmental, social, and governance factors affect investment decisions

## Exam Overview

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| **Format** | 100 multiple-choice questions |
| **Duration** | ~2 hours 20 minutes |
| **Delivery** | Online proctored (at home or at a test center) |
| **Passing score** | Not officially published; approximately 60% is widely cited |
| **Retake policy** | Multiple attempts allowed; check CFA Institute website for current rules |
| **Language** | English only |
| **Registration** | Via the CFA Institute official website |

> **Tip for beginners:** The exam tests understanding and application, not memorization. Focus on *why* ESG factors matter and *how* they are applied in investment analysis.

## Syllabus & Topic Areas

The curriculum is divided into **7 modules**. Below is a rough guide to their relative importance:

| # | Module | Approximate Weight |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Introduction to ESG Investing | ~10% |
| 2 | Environmental Factors | ~15–20% |
| 3 | Social Factors | ~15–20% |
| 4 | Governance Factors | ~15–20% |
| 5 | Engagement and Stewardship | ~10% |
| 6 | ESG Analysis, Valuation and Integration | ~15% |
| 7 | ESG Integrated Portfolio Construction and Management | ~10–15% |

### Module Summaries

**Module 1 — Introduction to ESG Investing**
Defines ESG, explains why it matters to investors, and introduces the key stakeholders (asset owners, managers, issuers, regulators). Covers the evolution from SRI (Socially Responsible Investing) to modern ESG integration.

**Module 2 — Environmental Factors**
Covers climate change, carbon emissions, energy transition, water scarcity, biodiversity, and the physical and transition risks companies face. Introduces frameworks like TCFD (Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures).

**Module 3 — Social Factors**
Examines labor practices, supply chain standards, human rights, product safety, community relations, and data privacy. Explains how social risks can affect company reputation, legal exposure, and long-term performance.

**Module 4 — Governance Factors**
Explores board structure and independence, executive compensation, shareholder rights, audit quality, anti-corruption policies, and corporate culture. Governance is the "G" but often the most measurable of the three pillars.

**Module 5 — Engagement and Stewardship**
Explains how investors influence company behavior through active ownership: voting on shareholder resolutions, direct engagement with management, and collaborative initiatives.

**Module 6 — ESG Analysis, Valuation and Integration**
Teaches how to incorporate ESG data into traditional financial analysis and valuation models. Covers ESG scoring methodologies, rating providers, and their limitations.

**Module 7 — ESG Integrated Portfolio Construction and Management**
Covers ESG approaches in different asset classes (equities, fixed income, real assets, alternatives) and how to build investment mandates that reflect ESG objectives.

## Study Plan

This plan is designed for **absolute beginners** with no prior finance or ESG background. Adjust the pace to fit your schedule.

> **Estimated total study time:** 80–120 hours over 8–12 weeks

### 8-Week Study Roadmap

```
Week 1-2  │  Foundation
          │  ├─ Module 1: Introduction to ESG
          │  ├─ Understand key ESG terms and frameworks
          │  └─ Read beginner-friendly ESG explainers online

Week 3-4  │  Environmental & Social Factors
          │  ├─ Module 2: Environmental Factors (TCFD, climate risk)
          │  └─ Module 3: Social Factors (labor, supply chain, privacy)

Week 5-6  │  Governance & Stewardship
          │  ├─ Module 4: Governance Factors
          │  └─ Module 5: Engagement and Stewardship

Week 7    │  Analysis & Portfolio Integration
          │  ├─ Module 6: ESG Analysis and Valuation
          │  └─ Module 7: Portfolio Construction with ESG

Week 8    │  Review & Practice
          │  ├─ Full curriculum review of weak areas
          │  ├─ Practice questions (aim for 200+ questions)
          │  └─ Simulate exam conditions with a timed mock
```

### Weekly Study Hours

| Week | Focus | Suggested Hours |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Read Module 1; build your ESG vocabulary | 8–10 hrs |
| 2 | Practice Module 1 questions; review terms | 6–8 hrs |
| 3 | Module 2 (Environmental) | 10–12 hrs |
| 4 | Module 3 (Social) | 10–12 hrs |
| 5 | Module 4 (Governance) | 10–12 hrs |
| 6 | Module 5 (Stewardship) | 8–10 hrs |
| 7 | Modules 6–7 (Analysis + Portfolio) | 12–15 hrs |
| 8 | Full review + mock exam | 10–12 hrs |

### Beginner Advice

- **Start with why:** Before diving into details, understand why ESG has become important to the financial industry. This makes the content stick much better.
- **Don't skip governance:** Module 4 is often the most exam-heavy in terms of testable details. Give it extra attention.
- **Use real examples:** When you read about a concept (e.g., carbon transition risk), search for a real company news story about it. Context makes abstract concepts concrete.
- **Practice over re-reading:** After your first pass of each module, switch to practice questions rather than re-reading the same text.

## How to Contribute

This is an open-source community project. Contributions of all kinds are welcome and encouraged!

### What you can contribute

- **Study notes** — your own summaries of any module
- **Practice questions** — original Q&A with explanations
- **Corrections** — fix errors or outdated information
- **Translations** — improve or expand the Chinese version
- **Exam experience** — share what the exam was actually like (without violating NDA)
- **Resources** — add useful links, tools, or communities you found helpful

### How to contribute (GitHub workflow)

1. Fork this repository
2. Create a new branch: `git checkout -b my-contribution`
3. Make your changes
4. Submit a Pull Request with a short description of what you added or changed

### Contribution guidelines

- Be respectful and constructive — this community exists to help beginners
- Do not share copyrighted material (e.g., verbatim curriculum text)
- Do not share specific exam questions from your actual exam (NDA applies)
- Keep information accurate — cite sources where possible
